Holiday Wine & Cheese Chill

Latkes on Hannukah. Fish head on Rosh HaShannah. The Jewish people do holidays right. But you don’t need to slow roast a brisket to throw a holiday gathering your friends will be talking about for months. Here’s the plan for a holiday dinner your bubbe could never imagine – unless your bubbe’s super cool. Think: Wine and cheese.

Follow this checklist and make it happen!

Choose an upcoming holiday

Choose a location

Come up with a title for the night (e.g. “Caroline’s Break Fast To End All Break Fasts”)

Make a guest list (and actually invite folks)

Buy lots of different cheeses and crackers (don’t forget the gluten free folks) and olives and grapes and other tasty/savory/fun morsels

Buy lots of WINE

Deck out your apartment really classy and creative: mood lighting, Jewish music from Spotify, pictures of great Jewish cultural heroes (jk), incense, etc.

Welcome guests – Tell them why this gathering is important to you

Perform any ritual acts associated with your particular holiday (e.g. say “Hamotzi” at a Break Fast, dip apples in honey for Rosh HaShannah, light a Menorah for Hannukah, etc.)

Continue EATING and DRINKING and LAUGHING

Consider deepening conversation by asking a “Jewish Question” that’s been on your mind – or choose from these “100 Prompts, Provocations, and Situations for Jewish Growth”
